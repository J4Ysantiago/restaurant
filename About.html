About Us <3

Our Roots

SoulTrain began as a humble home kitchen run by Mama Jenkins, a single mother with a talent for turning simple ingredients into soulful dishes that told stories of love, resilience, and heritage. Her cooking became a local sensation, inspiring her children to open a small diner in 1985, where the warmth of family, soulful music, and her cherished recipes created a welcoming space for all. As word spread, SoulTrain grew into a beloved franchise, blending its roots in Southern culinary tradition with a commitment to community and culture. Today, it’s a nationally recognized name, but each location remains true to its origins—serving comfort, connection, and history, one plate at a time.

Soul Goals

SoulTrain's goals focus on growth while preserving its roots. In the short term, it aims to enhance its brand, engage communities through events like "Soul Sundays," and standardize operations. Mid-term objectives include opening new locations, modernizing the menu with healthy options, and integrating technology for convenience. Long-term plans target national recognition, franchise expansion, and sustainability initiatives. Throughout, the mission remains to serve love, culture, and community through soulful food.

SoulTrain Top 5 Food facts!!!

1- Mama Jenkins’ Secret Ingredient: Legend has it that Mama Jenkins stirred every pot with her lucky wooden spoon, said to bring extra soul to the food!

2- Collard Green Champions: SoulTrain serves enough collard greens each year to fill an entire train car!

3- Mac 'n' Cheese Mania: The SoulTrain classic mac 'n' cheese uses a five-cheese blend, with just the right amount of "cheese pull" measured by Mama Jenkins’ original cheese ruler.

4- Cornbread Connoisseurs: Every piece of SoulTrain cornbread is baked with a heart-shaped imprint to symbolize the love in every meal.

5- Sweet Tea Secrets: SoulTrain’s sweet tea recipe was so closely guarded that Mama Jenkins wrote it in code, and only her family knows how to decode it.
